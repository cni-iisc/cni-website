Talks and Seminars

UGIF :- UI Grounded Instruction Following by Sagar Gubbi Venkatesh from Google
Dr Sagar talked about the natural language-based instruction following agent that helps new smartphone users learn how to use the phone on their own. The proposed system parses instructions from support sites using Large Language Models (LLMs) and generates macros which can be executed when the user asks queries.
Coding Schemes For Input-Constrained Channels by V. Arvind Rameshwar from IISc
The talk considered the construction of coding schemes over binary-input channels with input constraints. The focus was on two different channel models: random noise and adversarial noise channels. In each setting, the main questions sought to be addressed were the construction of explicit coding schemes and the derivation of good bounds on the rates achievable for a given amount of noise.

RF Beyond Communication:- New Opportunities for Human Sensing with mmWave by Sandip Chakraborty from IIT Kharagpur
In this talk, the speaker discussed how mmWave sensing could be leveraged to develop smart space, where the environment can sense the human beings within it and respond accordingly. He also discussed specific use cases, like how your car can seamlessly monitor the dangerous driving behaviours of its driver and respond accordingly without requiring any camera or sensors to be deployed on the driver’s body.

Finite time analysis of temporal difference learning with linear function approximation:- Tail averaging and regularisation by L A Prashanth from IIT Madras

The talk considered the finite-time behaviour of a popular temporal difference (TD) learning algorithm when combined with tail-averaging. The speaker also talked about the finite-time bounds on the parameter error of the tail-averaged TD iterate under a step size choice that does not require information about the eigenvalues of the underlying matrix used in the TD fixed point. The speaker suggests that future work should focus on extending the analysis to other TD algorithms, such as SARSA and Q-learning.
